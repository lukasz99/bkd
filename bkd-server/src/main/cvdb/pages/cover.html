Welcome to Channel Variants of Unknown Significance (CVUS) Database
The CVUS knowledgebase provides a tool to assess variants in cellular ion channel genes and transporters. Pathogenic variants or mutations in many of these genes disrupt the function of the channel and are associated with human disease such as epilepsy or cardiac arrhythmia, among others. Genetic sequencing can also identify variants of uncertain significance (VUS) in these same genes. Providing information on if the potentially modified protein affects function can be valuable to determining if a given variant is detrimental. CVUS provides data for improved variant classification that can aid computational and biophysics-driven approaches to assessing ion channels and transporters and how they impact human disease.
The database currently covers:
Voltage Gated Potassium Channels:
