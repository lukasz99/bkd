Welcome to Channel Variants of Unknown Significance (CVUS) KnowledgeBase

  
The CVUS KnowledgeBase provides a tool to assess variants in cellular ion channel genes and transporters. Pathogenic variants or mutations in many of these genes disrupt the function of the channel and are associated with human disease such as epilepsy or cardiac arrhythmia, among others. CVUS KB keeps track of the variants reported in the public databases (ClinVar and dbSNP) and presents them in the context of genomic, proteomic and evolutionary data.

Sequencing of patients' genomes can also identify not yet characterized, novel variants of uncertain significance (VUS) in these same genes. Providing information if the potentially modified protein affects function can be valuable to determining if a given variant is detrimental and might suggest potential therapies.

CVUS KnowledgeBase combines public information retrieved from ClinVar, dbSNP and UniprotKB databases with locally-generated data (blue arrows) in order to improve classification of newly discovered gene/protein variants. It provides a central access point to information accessible from numerous public resources and can aid computational and biophysics-driven approaches to assessing function in the modified ion channels and transporters and how they impact human disease.
